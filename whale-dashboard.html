Scans each ticker across all four whale sources and scores how strongly they align. Run the scan to begin.
Methodology — weighted components: Options flow 35 (today's whale prints; direction = call vs put premium tilt) · Insiders 30 (Form 4 filings over the lookback; open-market buys weighted heaviest — the best-documented signal; sales weighted low, they're usually noise) · Institutions 20 (latest 13F holdings of 12 tracked funds; put positions count as bearish; quarterly data with up to 45-day lag — positioning, not timing) · Congress 15 (STOCK Act disclosures, up to 45-day reporting lag; requires Finnhub key, otherwise excluded from the score). Score = points earned ÷ points available from active sources, minus itemized cross-examination deductions (evidence opposing the prevailing direction: insider sales against bullish flow, fund puts, quarter-over-quarter trims/exits — capped at 20 pts, every deduction shown). CONFLUENCE badge = 2+ sources active on a ticker, STRONG = 3+.

Theses & ledger: each directional row becomes a falsifiable claim — outperform/underperform SPY over a fixed horizon (14d default, 21d for strong confluence, 30d when insiders dominate, since insider alpha is slower), with an objective invalidation level taken from the whale's own strike when available. "Log theses" snapshots entry prices; after each deadline the dashboard grades itself against real prices and shows its cumulative hit rate and average excess return — including losses. Market odds lines are risk-neutral probabilities implied by the whale contract's own IV (Black-Scholes N(d₂)) — what the options market itself believes, as a reality check on the signal. Data lags still apply (13F quarterly, congress up to 45d). This is a research instrument, not trade advice — judge it by its ledger, and verify filings via the links in each tab.
