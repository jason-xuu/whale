Scans each ticker across all four whale sources and scores how strongly they align. Runs automatically when you open this tab, and re-runs on the auto-refresh interval.
Methodology — weighted components: Options flow 35 (today's whale prints; direction = call vs put premium tilt) · Insiders 30 (Form 4 filings over the lookback; open-market buys weighted heaviest — the best-documented signal; sales weighted low, they're usually noise) · Institutions 20 (latest 13F holdings of 12 tracked funds; put positions count as bearish; quarterly data with up to 45-day lag — positioning, not timing) · Congress 15 (STOCK Act disclosures, up to 45-day reporting lag; requires Finnhub key, otherwise excluded from the score). Score = points earned ÷ points available from active sources, minus itemized cross-examination deductions (evidence opposing the prevailing direction: insider sales against bullish flow, fund puts, quarter-over-quarter trims/exits — capped at 20 pts, every deduction shown). CONFLUENCE badge = 2+ sources active on a ticker, STRONG = 3+.

Theses & ledger: each directional row becomes a falsifiable claim — outperform/underperform SPY over a fixed horizon (14d default, 21d for strong confluence, 30d when insiders dominate, since insider alpha is slower), with an objective invalidation level taken from the whale's own strike when available. "Log theses" snapshots entry prices; after each deadline the dashboard grades itself against real prices and shows its cumulative hit rate and average excess return — including losses. Market odds lines are risk-neutral probabilities implied by the whale contract's own IV (Black-Scholes N(d₂)) — what the options market itself believes, as a reality check on the signal. Microstructure. Flow direction is read the way a desk reads it, not by assuming every call is bullish: trades at the ask are classified buyer-initiated and at the bid seller-initiated; the tape's net delta-adjusted dollar flow is computed across the whole chain; dealer gamma exposure (Black-Scholes gamma from each contract's own IV × open interest) flags pinning vs amplification regimes; 25Δ skew and IV term structure enter the cross-exam. Each ticker also builds a private baseline of these metrics from your own scans (stored locally, one point per day) — after 5 scan-days, today's readings get z-scores against that history, because "unusual" only means something relative to a name's own normal. Confidence. Every verdict carries a HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW confidence tier from source count, score-weighted directional agreement, evidence level, and how much the cross-exam took back. Insider buys are conviction-weighted (C-suite buys and ≥3-buyer clusters count extra); 13F positions are weighted by their share of each fund's book, not just dollars. Recommended move lines are derived entirely from the scan: direction from the verdict; the contract by execution-quality scoring of aligned whale prints (horizon coverage, delta band, bid/ask spread, open interest, aggressor side — a print the whale sold is never mirrored, and if every aligned print traded at the bid the engine says so and routes to shares); cost/breakeven from the contract itself; the edge test compares the market's implied past-breakeven odds against this ledger's own graded hit rate — once ≥10 theses are graded, positive edge produces a half-Kelly risk budget (capped 5%), and no edge says so plainly. Rich front IV triggers a debit-spread alternative priced from real legs in the same chain. When signals are mixed or weak, the recommendation is to stand aside.
